Members
We are second-year Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering (BSCpE) students whose studies include computer systems, software, hardware, and the fundamental principles that power modern computing technologies. Through academic coursework, technical projects, and collaborative learning experiences, we continuously develop the knowledge and skills needed to understand how computers and digital systems function.

As part of our academic journey, we created OSphere to reflect our growing interest in Operating Systems and their essential role in computing. Operating systems serve as the foundation that connects hardware and software, managing system resources and providing the environment in which applications run. By studying these concepts, we gain a deeper appreciation of the mechanisms that make modern computing possible and the technologies that shape our digital world.
About Us
Objectives OSphere was created to provide a centralized and accessible platform for learning Operating System concepts. We aim to present technical topics in a clear, organized, and student-friendly manner, making them easier to understand and explore. Through this platform, we hope to support students in developing a stronger understanding of how operating systems function and their importance in modern computing. By bringing together essential concepts and learning resources, OSphere seeks to encourage continuous learning and a deeper appreciation of the systems that power today's digital technologies.
